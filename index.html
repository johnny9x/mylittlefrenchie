The Breed

With a tough-on-the-outside, sweet-on-the-inside demeanor, unmistakable bat-shaped ears and distinctive bow-legged gait, the French Bulldog has gained so much popularity that they're fast becoming the city-dwellers' dog of choice. They're small, under 28 pounds, and have a short easy-care coat that comes in a variety of colors. They don't need a great deal of exercise, fit comfortably into a condo or apartment, and are far less likely to bark than many small dogs. In fact, other than being a little pugnacious with other dogs, it would be hard to imagine a better dog for city living. --www.vetstreet.com

Care and Training

The French Bulldog has a short, fine, smooth coat that is easy to groom. Brush him weekly with a rubber hound glove or a soft bristle brush. Bathe monthly or as needed to keep the coat clean.

What we believe

All our dogs are raised in the home. There is no kennel or caging of any kind. When the puppies are young, they are kept in a crated area in our kitchen for their safety. Once they are able to walk around and play, they are free to roam around the house with our other pets and children. This means that all the puppies come socialized with dogs, cats, people and kids!

All our puppies are part of our extended family. We miss them once they are gone so we have puppy play dates twice a year for everyone to get together, play and catch up. We love seeing our puppies grow up and become part of their new family.

How we raise our puppies

Our puppies are also part of the family. They are raised in the home with our other dogs, cats and children. They are spoiled with love and attention. They stay with their mother every day until 8 weeks of age when they are able to transition smoothly to their new home. By 4-5 weeks, they begin having outside time to play and begin potty training. All of our puppies come with a one year health guarantee. They are all vet checked and come microchipped, de-wormed and with the first series of shots completed.

Meet our dogs

We take excellent care of our dogs. They are fed high quality food, exercised daily, and sleep in the house every night. They are veterinary checked twice yearly. Our females are only bred once a year. Lucca was bred 3 times and was a great mom. She was then spayed and is now just part of the family. Luna is our only breeding female at this time. Her first litter is due November 2014. As you can see, our dogs are more than just dogs, they are family.
